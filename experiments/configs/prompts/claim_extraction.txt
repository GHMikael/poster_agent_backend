paper-grounded.

# RULES

1. Each claim is ONE declarative sentence stating a single fact the paper makes.
   GOOD: "BLIP achieves 87.8% accuracy on the COCO benchmark."
   BAD : "BLIP is a great model that achieves high accuracy across many tasks."
2. Atomicity: split compound sentences. "X improves Y by 5% and Z by 3%"
   becomes two claims, not one.
3. Provenance: ``section`` ∈ {Motivation, Problem, Method, Experiments,
   Results, Conclusion}. Use "Other" if none fits.
4. Don't include the paper's contribution list verbatim — abstract those
   into specific factual statements.
5. Do NOT invent or paraphrase to add hedges/qualifiers the paper doesn't say.
6. Target {target_n} claims total. Stop early if the paper genuinely has less.
7. claim_id is "c-NNN" zero-padded, starting from c-001.

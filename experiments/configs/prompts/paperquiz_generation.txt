You are an academic paper exam-question writer. Read the paper text and
produce a small set of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) that test
*core* paper knowledge — not formatting, page numbers, or trivia.

# OUTPUT FORMAT (JSON only — no Markdown)

{
  "mcqs": [
    {
      "question_id": "q-001",
      "question": "<one sentence; specific, paper-grounded>",
      "options": {"A": "<option>", "B": "<option>", "C": "<option>", "D": "<option>"},
      "correct": "B",
      "section": "Method"
    },
    ...
  ]
}

# RULES

1. Produce exactly {n_questions} MCQs.
2. Each question has 4 options A/B/C/D and exactly one correct.
3. Distractors must be plausible (the paper says something related but not
   identical), not absurd.
4. Cover diverse sections: at least one from each of {Motivation, Problem,
   Method, Experiments / Results, Conclusion} if possible.
5. Questions are answerable from the paper alone (no external knowledge).
6. Avoid yes/no questions and "all of the above".
7. ``section`` field marks the paper section the question is grounded in.

# PAPER TEXT (truncated)
{paper_text}
